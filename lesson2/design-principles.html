White Space and Clean Design

  • Name:Time Magazine
  • URL:https://www.time.com

Proximity is the website's use of whitespaces to make the text visually appealling and easy to read. The element of good design is to have less text and use more visuals to place appropriately within and around whaite space to create a clean look and feel. Time Magazines website does this very effectively with lots of orderly space making the site easy to read.

PARC: Proximity

  • Name: The British Broadcasting Corporation
  • URL:https://www.bbc.com

According to this principle the closeness of items on a website helps the site create a narrative and an association between them. Similar things like the menu are placed toggether to create an association. The BBC site does this very well with similar news items placed together and grouped to create and associate and drive a narrative. Sports news, African News, UK News and other articles are clearly grouped to gether.

Hicks Law

  • Name: First National Bank, South Africa
  • URL: https://www.fnb.co.za/

According to Hick's law every additional choice increases the time required to take a decision. FNB website examplified the design principle by the way it clearly narrows the options that a visitor has. There is no endless scroll that would drive a visitor to keep scrolling down in anticipation of more possible choices to evaluate before making a decision. Rather a clear bulleted icon list of narrow options is provided for the visitor. And once the visitor clicks on one of the options the screen refreshes, wipes out all other options from the page and presents another narrow checkerboard group of options focused only on the option the user has selected. For example if a visitor selects "invest" all other menu icons disappear and the user is immidiately shown only investment options. By this way the visitor is quickly driven to a decision on what he wants to do.
